FAQ

Questions people
always ask.

Everything you need to know before booking a call.

Do I need both the website and AI automation, or can I just get one?+
Absolutely, you can start with just a website and add automation later when you are ready. Most clients start with the website only. There is no pressure to do both at once.
What do I need to provide to get started?+
Just your business name, what you do, who your customers are, and any logos or photos you have. I handle everything else, design, copy structure, hosting setup, and launch. We go over all of this on the initial call.
What if I don't like what you build?+
Every project includes two full rounds of revisions. If after both rounds you are still not happy, you pay nothing. I only want to be paid for work you are proud of.
How does payment work?+
50% upfront to begin the project, 50% on delivery. Payment via Stripe card or bank transfer. For pro bono projects there is no payment, just a written testimonial at the end.
Can you work with my existing domain or website?+
Yes. If you already have a domain I can connect the new site to it. If you have an existing site I can rebuild it or redesign specific pages. We figure out the best approach on the initial call.
What happens after my site launches?+
You receive all files and full ownership of everything I built. I stay reachable by email for 30 days after launch for any small fixes or questions. Ongoing maintenance or updates can be arranged separately.
Will my site show up on Google?+
Every site I build includes SEO basics, title tags, meta descriptions, keyword placement, and image alt text. I also provide guidance on setting up your Google Business profile. Full SEO is a longer process but I give you the right foundation from day one.
How are you different from other web designers?+
I write real code, no Wix, no Squarespace, no templates. That means faster sites, cleaner design, and no monthly platform fees. I also understand business, not just technology. And you work directly with me, not an account manager passing messages to a team you never meet.
